About Herbalism

“For years, plant-based remedies have been used as a traditional practice to treat and prevent a broad range of diseases.

During the past decade, natural therapies have regained public attention..."

REF: Capasso, R., Lucariello, G. and Cicia, D. eds., (2021). Natural Medicine in Therapy..



I was inspired by my Romanian culture and folk traditions, which still use herbalism passed down by our ancestors.

Historical records by Herodotus, Hippocrates, Galen, and Dioscorides mention the Dacians’ use of medicinal plants, showing how advanced their herbal medicine was.

Dacian healers used many herbs for their healing properties, and these practices have been handed down through generations.


Since I was a child, I loved spending time in nature, collecting herbs like wild river mint to make tea at home.

My passion for nature has been the cornerstone of my life for the past decade.

Over these 10 years, I have immersed myself in exploring and experimenting with the benefits of natural herbs and flowers.

Tending to my own garden, I have learned firsthand the art of cultivating, collecting, drying, and storing a wide array of herbs.

Each year, as I harvest nature’s bounty, I find immense joy in brewing a perfect cup of herbal tea—a simple pleasure that nourishes both body and spirit.

This personal journey has not only deepened my understanding of natural remedies but has also fueled my desire to share this knowledge with others.


My project is a reflection of this lifelong passion, designed to educate and inspire individuals about the healing power of nature, one herb and one brew at a time.

My favorite herbs are chamomile and mint because they have helped me with digestive problems.

I am still learning and discovering new herbs and plants every day.
